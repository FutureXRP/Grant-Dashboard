# Section 13 — Marketing, Communications, Brand Strategy & Donor Storytelling

> **Purpose**
>
> Every nonprofit has two stories.
>
> The first is the work it does.
>
> The second is the story people remember.
>
> The organizations that attract major donors, secure transformational grants, recruit exceptional staff, and influence public policy are almost always excellent communicators.
>
> This section establishes a comprehensive communications framework that enables SquareOne Compassion to consistently tell its story with clarity, authenticity, and measurable impact while strengthening public trust and advancing its mission.

---

# Communications Philosophy

Marketing for a nonprofit is not about promotion.

It is about stewardship.

Every communication should answer one or more of the following questions:

- Who are we?
- Why do we exist?
- Who do we serve?
- What changed?
- Why should someone care?
- How can others become involved?

The objective is not attention.

The objective is trust.

---

# Brand Promise

Every public communication should reinforce a single organizational promise.

> **SquareOne Compassion exists to strengthen families and communities through accessible healthcare, early childhood education, wellness, and community-centered services delivered with compassion, excellence, and integrity.**

This message should remain consistent regardless of audience.

---

# Ethical Storytelling

Protect the dignity and privacy of participants.

Obtain appropriate written consent before using identifiable stories, photographs, or testimonials.

Avoid:

- Exploiting hardship
- Sharing unnecessary personal details
- Misrepresenting outcomes
- Using images without permission
- Revealing protected health or educational information

# Fundraising Philosophy

Fundraising is not asking people for money.

Donors do not invest in organizations.

They invest in outcomes.

They invest in leadership.

The objective is to build lifelong partners rather than one-time contributors.
